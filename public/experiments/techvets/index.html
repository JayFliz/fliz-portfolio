What is this?
The Transition Unit
system explained

Transition Unit is a structured guidance tool — not a training platform itself, but a system that connects veterans and service leavers to the right pathways and training resources in the right order.

The core insight: the problem is not a lack of resources. There are excellent free training partners available. The problem is structure — knowing what to do, in what order, and why.

How it works

1. Choose your career path
Select from five tech disciplines, or use the Path Finder quiz to get a recommendation based on your background and interests.
2. Follow your Mission Track
Each path has four phases: Foundation → Skills → Application → Job Readiness. Each phase has clear tasks and recommended training.
3. Access partner training
Integrated links to TechVets training partners — Skillsoft, Cisco, Immersive Labs, and more — at the right phase in your track.
4. Reach job readiness
Exit with a completed CV, portfolio or certification, and a clear narrative for civilian employers. Apply via TechVets Employment Support.

Who is this for?

Veterans, service leavers, reservists, and military spouses and partners who want to move into technology careers. No prior tech experience required to start — every path has a Foundation phase that assumes no background.

Is this free?

Yes. This tool is free to use. The training partners linked throughout offer free or significantly subsidised access for the military community. Some certifications may have costs — these are clearly indicated.

Who built this?

This is a concept developed in partnership with TechVets and Forces Employment Charity. It is a navigable prototype designed to demonstrate how a structured transition system could work without requiring login or data collection.
