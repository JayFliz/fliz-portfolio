Open Sources for the Intermediate Command and Staff Course (Land)

What you described is essentially the UK Intermediate Command and Staff Course (Land), a sensitive professional military education programme. There is no public, complete course pack or full reading list, but there are credible open-source equivalents and feeder materials used across UK and NATO-aligned staff education.

Important: this document points only to open, public sources. It does not include restricted teaching material, current internal exercises, assessment material, or operationally sensitive content.

1. Official and Semi-Official Defence Sources

These are the closest public equivalents to primary course material.

  • UK Defence Doctrine (JDP 0-01) — the main UK capstone doctrine publication.
  • Joint Doctrine Publications (JDPs) collection — gateway to the wider UK doctrine library.
  • Joint Concept Note 1/20: Multi-Domain Integration — useful for the multi-domain integration side of the syllabus.
  • The Strategic Defence Review 2025 — current strategic policy context for UK defence.
  • Integrated Operating Concept — historically important, but note that this GOV.UK publication is marked withdrawn.

2. NATO Doctrine and Planning Sources

These are especially useful for operational planning, staff work, and multinational interoperability.

  • AJP-3: Allied Joint Doctrine for the Conduct of Operations
  • Public Affairs Handbook (contains references to AJP-5 and the planning process)
  • NATO Joint Warfare Centre: Doctrine Development Process
  • NATO Lessons Learned Handbook
  • AJP-3.9: Allied Joint Doctrine for Joint Targeting

Note: public access to the latest edition of AJP-5 is inconsistent, so the links above are safe public proxies for NATO planning doctrine and practice.

5. Allied Staff College Material

Your description mentions the two-week exercise at the US Command and General Staff College, Fort Leavenworth. For public, related context, start with the institution itself:

  • US Command and General Staff College

From there, public material on staff planning, orders, and military decision-making can usually be found through Army University and Fort Leavenworth resources.

6. Historical Campaign and Staff Ride Sources (Normandy)

Your course description explicitly references Operation OVERLORD, including DEADSTICK, GOODWOOD, and TRACTABLE.

  • Imperial War Museums: Scenes from Operation Goodwood

  • Imperial War Museums: Scenes on the second day of Operation Goodwood
  • Imperial War Museums: The 11th Armoured Division in action on Operation Goodwood

7. What You Are Unlikely to Find Publicly

  • Actual syndicate room material
  • Current internal MAPEX, MODELEX, or TEWT packs
  • Real assessment rubrics and staff grading material
  • Current operational scenarios or restricted doctrine supplements

That said, a large share of the intellectual content behind the course can still be studied through doctrine, academic reading, campaign history, and public staff-college material.

8. Practical Self-Study Sequence

  1. Start with UK Defence Doctrine (JDP 0-01).
  2. Move to Multi-Domain Integration and related UK concepts.
  3. Read public NATO planning and operations doctrine such as AJP-3.
  4. Use King's College London material for conflict, strategy, and security studies.
  5. Use Henley material for leadership, management, and executive-level thinking.
  6. Study Normandy campaign sources to mirror the staff ride and historical analysis components.
